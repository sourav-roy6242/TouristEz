BUXA

Buxa Tiger Reserve is a wildlife sanctuary located in the Buxa hills of the Eastern Himalayas in West Bengal, India. The reserve is famous for its diverse wildlife, including the Royal Bengal Tiger, Asian elephant, Indian rhinoceros, clouded leopard, and many other species of mammals, reptiles, and birds. The Buxa hills are also home to several indigenous tribes, including the Lepchas, Rajbanshis, and Bhutias

HOW TO GO

Take a train from Howrah to Alipurduar Junction, and then take a taxi or bus to Buxa Tiger Reserve.

Jaldapara National Park

Jaldapara National Park is a wildlife sanctuary located in the Alipurduar district of West Bengal, India. It is famous for its population of one-horned Indian rhinoceroses. The best time to visit is between October and May when the weather is pleasant and the wildlife is most active. However, the monsoon season from June to September can also be a great time to visit, as the forest is lush and green during this time.

HOW TO GO

Take a train from Howrah to New Jalpaiguri Station, and then take a taxi or bus to Jaldapara National Park.

Sundarbans National Park

Sundarbans National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the delta region of the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers in India and Bangladesh. The park is famous for its mangrove forests, which are the largest in the world and provide a unique habitat for a wide variety of flora and fauna. It is home to the Royal Bengal Tiger, estuarine crocodile, Indian python, spotted deer, and many species of birds, including the endangered white-bellied sea eagle. The best time to visit is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and the wildlife is most active. However, the park is closed during the monsoon season from June to September due to heavy rainfall and flooding.

HOW TO GO

Take a train from Howrah to Canning Station, and then take a boat to Sundarbans National Park.

Neora Valley National Park

Neora Valley National Park is a protected area located in the Kalimpong district of West Bengal, India. The park is situated in the Eastern Himalayas and is famous for its unique ecosystems, which include dense forests, rolling hills, and meadows. It is home to several endangered species, including the red panda, clouded leopard, Asiatic black bear, and many species of birds. The best time to visit is between October and May when the weather is pleasant and the wildlife is most active.

HOW TO GO

Take a train from Howrah to New Mal Junction, and then take a taxi or bus to Neora Valley National Park.

.Singalila National Park

Singalila National Park is a protected area located in the Darjeeling district of West Bengal, India. The park is situated at an altitude of 7000 feet above sea level and is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including the red panda, Himalayan black bear, clouded leopard, and many species of birds. The park is also famous for its rhododendron forests, which bloom in the spring and create a beautiful display of colors. The best time to visit Singalila National Park is between March and May when the weather is pleasant and the rhododendrons are in full bloom

HOW TO GO

Take a train from Howrah to New Jalpaiguri Station, and then take a taxi or bus to Singalila National Park

Mahananda Wildlife Sanctuary

Chapramari Wildlife Sanctuary

Chapramari Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area located in the Jalpaiguri district of West Bengal, India. The sanctuary is situated on the banks of the Murti River and is home to several species of animals, including the Indian rhinoceros, Indian elephant, Bengal tiger, and many species of birds. The sanctuary is also famous for its lush green forests, rolling hills, and beautiful meadows. The sanctuary is also an important corridor for the movement of elephants and other animals between the Buxa and Gorumara National Parks. The best time to visit Chapramari Wildlife Sanctuary is between October and May when the weather is pleasant and the wildlife is most active.

HOW TO GO

Take a train from Howrah to New Jalpaiguri Station, and then take a taxi or bus to Chapramari Wildlife Sanctuary

Ballavpur Wildlife Sanctuary

This wildlife sanctuary is located in Bolpur Santiniketan. This Wildlife Sanctuary is home to three large water bodies (Jheel) which hosts migratory as well as resident birds in plenty. It is home to numerous Cheethals (Spotted Deer), Blackbuck and other resident animals like jackals and foxes.

HOW TO GO

The nearest railway station is Bolpur-Santiniketan. You can get many Trains from Howrah and Sealdah railway stations. After reaching Bolpur-Santiniketan station, you need to take a local Toto, Rickshaw to reach the exact location.

Bhutanghat Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in Jalpaiguri district of West Bengal, Bhutanghat is situated in the mountainous terrain along the river Raidak near the Indo-Bhutan border and is around 45 km from Alipurduar. Known for its pristine evergreen forests, rolling tea gardens and mesmerizing natural beauty, this picturesque little hamlet is home to many rare animals, including the one-horned Asiatic rhino, leopards and spotted deer, making it a must-visit offbeat destination for all wildlife aficionados.

HOW TO GO

Take a train from Howrah to New Mal Junction, and then take a taxi or bus to Bhutanghat Wildlife Sanctuary.
