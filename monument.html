VICTORIA MEMORIAL

It is dedicated to the memory of Queen Victoria, Empress of India from 1876 to 1901. The largest monument to a monarch anywhere in the world, it stands in 64 acres of gardens and is now a museum under the control of the Ministry of Culture

HOW TO GO There is a direct bus departing from Vidyasagar Setu and arriving at Rabindra Sadan. Services depart every 15 minutes, and operate every day. The journey takes approximately 11 min. The distance between Howrah and Victoria Memorial is 4 km. The best way to get from Howrah to Victoria Memorial without a car is to bus which takes 23 min and costs ₹20 - ₹30.

Dakshineshwar Kali Temple

Dakshineswar Kali Temple is situated on the banks of the Hooghly River, the Dakshineshwar Kali Temple is one of the famous places of worship not only in Kolkata but all in the whole east India. Rani Rashmoni founded the temple in 1855, a philanthropist and an ardent devotee of goddess Kali. The presiding beauty and belief of the temple is Bhavatarini, an aspect of goddess Kali. The 19th-century mystic sage and reformer Ramakrishna Paramhansa and his wife Sarada Devi were attached to this temple.

HOW TO GO There is a direct bus departing from Babughat and arriving at Dakhineswar. Services depart every 30 minutes, and operate every day. The journey takes approximately 30 min. There is also a direct train departing from Esplanade and arriving at Dakshineswar. Services depart every 10 minutes, and operate every day. The journey takes approximately 29 min. The best way to get from Howrah to Dakshineswar without a car is to Metro which takes 29 min and costs ₹16 - ₹20.

FORT WILLIAM

Fort William, citadel of Calcutta (now Kolkata), named for King William III of England. The British East India Company’s main Bengal trading station was moved from Hooghly (now Hugli) to Calcutta in 1690 after a war with the Mughals. Between 1696 and 1702 a fort was built in Calcutta, with the nawab (ruler) of Bengal’s permission.

HOW TO GO The cheapest way to get from Howrah to Fort William (Station) is to bus and line 36 tram which costs ₹28 - ₹45 and takes 32 min. The distance between Howrah and Fort William (Station) is 4 km. It takes approximately 32 min to get from Howrah to Fort William (Station), including transfers.

Writer’s Building

Designed by Thomas Lyon, a self- styled builder, the Writer's building was constructed in 1777 on behalf of Richard Barwell. The building was meant for the East India Company to house the junior level servants who were then called 'Writers' and hence, the name of the building. The building is built with red brick in Greco-Roman neo-classical style, where are 13 Blocks, Porch, Long iron staircases, Corinthian column & Corridor. The walls of corridor contain various colonial-style statues, sculptures and paintings.

HOW TO GO The distance between Howrah Railway Station to Writers Building is 4 Km by road. You can also find the distance from Howrah Railway Station to Writers Building using other travel options like bus, subway, tram, train and rail. Apart from the trip distance, refer Directions from Howrah Railway Station to Writers Building for road driving directions.

Shaheed Minar is an outstanding monument of Bangladesh. It was built to tribute the martyrs who given up their life for language. The main incident had been occurring inside of Dhaka medical college hospital. So a decision was taken to build a memorial adjacent to DMCH.

Indian Meuseum

The Indian Museum is the largest and oldest museum in India and has rare collections of antiques, armour and ornaments, fossils, skeletons, mummies, and Mughal paintings. What Makes It Awesome. The Indian Museum is the largest and oldest museum in the entire Asia-Pacific region. Be prepared to get enthralled by over 100,000 rare exhibits comprising antiques, armour, ornaments, fossils, skeletons, mummies and paintings.

HOW TO GO There is a direct bus departing from Vidyasagar Setu and arriving at Park Street. Services depart every 30 minutes, and operate every day. The journey takes approximately 17 min. The distance between Howrah and Indian Museum is 4 km. Howrah to Indian Museum bus services, operated by CSTC, depart from Vidyasagar Setu station. The bus from Vidyasagar Setu to Park Street takes 17 min including transfers and departs every 30 minutes.

Cooch Behar Palace

Also known as the “Rajbari”, Cooch Behar Palace was designed on the model of London's Buckingham Palace in the year 1887 during the reign of Maharaja Nripendra Narayan. The Cooch Behar Palace is renowned for its elegance and grandeur of the rich royal heritage of this region.

HOW TO GO The cheapest way to get from Howrah to Cooch Behar is to bus which costs ₹850 - ₹1,000 and takes 18h 13m. There is a direct train departing from Howrah Jn and arriving at New Cooch Behar. Services depart twice daily, and operate every day. The journey takes approximately 11h 35m. The distance between Howrah and Cooch Behar is 432 km. The road distance is 580.7 km. It takes approximately 4h 22m to get from Howrah to Cooch Behar, including transfers.

Howrah Bridge

An iconic landmark in Kolkata, the Howrah Bridge is a huge steel bridge over the Hooghly River. It is considered to be one of the longest cantilever bridges in the world. Also known as Rabindra Setu, it connects Howrah and Kolkata. It carries 100,000 vehicles and countless pedestrians daily.

HOW TO GO The distance between Howrah Station and Howrah Bridge is 333 meters. The quickest way to get from Howrah Station to Howrah Bridge is to bus which costs ₹15 - ₹22 and takes 1 min.

Hazarduari Palace

Hazarduari Palace is famous for having a thousand entrances. The palace contains just 100 actual doors, with the remaining 900 being fake to throw off intruders. The palace was built by Duncun McLeod for the Nawab Najim Humaun Jah, descendent of Mir Zafar. The palace was used for holding meetings as well as for accommodation of high British officials. The palace is now a museum and displays collection of armoury, paintings and portraits of the Nawab..The Hazarduari Palace Museum is a famous tourist attraction exhibiting beautiful artefacts, paintings, weapons like Siraj-Ud-Daula's swords, statues and vintage cars that once belonged to the Nawabs of the 18th century.

HOW TO GO The cheapest way to get from Howrah to Hazarduari Palace is to train which costs ₹220 - ₹1,400 and takes 5h 2m. there is no direct train from Howrah to Hazarduari Palace. However, there are services departing from Sealdah and arriving at Murshidabad via Ranaghat Jn. The journey, including transfers, takes approximately 5h 2m.

St. Paul’s Cathedral

It is said to be the largest church in Kolkata and the first Anglican cathedral in Asia. It was also the first new-built cathedral in the overseas territory of the British Empire.St Paul's Cathedral, Kolkata, India. Architect: William Nairn Forbes (1796-1855), with C. K. Robinson, who was one of the Magistrates of Calcutta.

HOW TO GO The cheapest way to get from Howrah to St. Paul's Cathedral is to bus which costs ₹20 - ₹30 and takes 19 min. There is a direct bus departing from Vidyasagar Setu and arriving at Rabindra Sadan. Services depart every 15 minutes, and operate every day. The journey takes approximately 11 min. The distance between Howrah and St. Paul's Cathedral is 5 km.
